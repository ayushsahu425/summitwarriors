Bagini Glacier: A Hidden Himalayan Gem

14,816 FT

Max Altitude

8 Days

Duration

Moderate-Hard

Difficulty

Located in the Chamoli district within the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve, the Bagini Glacier trek offers a raw, less-crowded alternative to mainstream trails. It provides unparalleled close-up views of iconic peaks like Changabang (6866m), Kalanka (6931m), and Dronagiri. This is a journey through traditional villages and vast glacial moraines.

Expedition Itinerary

1
Rishikesh to Joshimath

Drive via the confluences of the Alaknanda river. Evening briefing and final gear check in Joshimath.

2
Jumma to Ruing

Short drive to Jumma followed by a trek through beautiful forest trails to reach the Ruing campsite.

3
Ruing to Dronagiri Village

A steady climb through alpine meadows to Dronagiri village—a place steeped in mythology where Lord Hanuman is said to have lifted the Sanjeevani hill.

4
Dronagiri to Longatulli

The valley opens up, revealing the massive glacial landscape. Witness the forked peaks of Garur and the granite walls of Changabang.

5
Bagini Base Camp

Trek towards the snout of the glacier. Establish base camp at an altitude offering panoramic views of the high peaks.

6
Summit & Glacier Exploration

Visit the main glacier viewpoint. Experience the sheer scale of the Himalayan giants and the Changabang base area.

7
Return to Dronagiri

Trek back from the glacier base to Dronagiri village. Evening rest in homestays or tents.

8
Final Descent & Drive

Trek from Dronagiri to Jumma, followed by a drive back to Rishikesh where the expedition concludes.
